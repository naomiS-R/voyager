Betelgeuse

Other names:

Alpha Orionis

Facts:

  • It is the ninth-brightest star in the night sky and second-brightest in the constellation of Orion.
  • It is a red supergiant, and is expercted to explode as a supernova within the next million years.
  • If Betelgeuse were at the center of the Solar System, its surface would extend past the asteroid belt, wholly engulfing the orbits of Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, and possibly Jupiter. Calculations of its mass range from slightly under ten to a little over twenty times that of the Sun.
