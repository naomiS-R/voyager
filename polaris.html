Polaris

Other names:

Alpha Ursae Minoris, the North Star or Pole Star

Facts:

  • Polaris is a multiple star, comprising of the main star (Polaris Aa, a yellow supergiant) in orbit with a smaller companion (Polaris Ab); the pair in orbit with Polaris B.
  • It is the brightest star in the constellation of Ursa Minor.
  • It is very close to the north celestial pole, making it the current northern pole star.
  • The distance to Polaris is about 433 light-years
